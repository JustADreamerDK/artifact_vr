Science behind

  • Exposure therapy is a effective technique used for reducing and anxiety through exposure to the patient’s triggers.
  • Virtual Reality can be used to create scenarios, wherein the patient can face their anxiety
  • Research has shown that using Virtual Reality to supplement regular exposure therapy sees an increase in effectivity over the regular treatment.
  • Using VR, it is easier to secure that the patient doesn’t experience anything that is too intense, when facing a scenario that would otherwise induce anxiety.
  • When confronting the source of anxiety in a safe environment, the brain will gradually that it is not in any actual danger, which results in a lowered to eliminated anxious response.
  • The exercise can easily be interrupted, if the patient experiences anything that is too intense. Yet it is still easy to increase the level of difficulty, when the patient feels ready for the next step.
  • A study from Canada of 59 adults diagnosed with social anxiety, discovered that when facing the source of anxiety with virtual reality, the treatment was more effective and practical while still being cheaper than the traditional exposure therapy sessions. 76,5 percent of the patients experienced a reductions of symptoms, with the effects continuing for at least 6 months. /li>
